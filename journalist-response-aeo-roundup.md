# Response: AEO Roundup Pitch

Hi there,

Quinn Schwartz here. I'm the founder of Schwartz Marketing Lab, an SEO, AEO, and content marketing agency working with B2B and SaaS companies, including Nooks and Search Party, on organic growth and AI visibility. Happy to contribute. Answers below, use whatever's useful.

**How does AEO differ from traditional SEO in practice?**

Traditional SEO optimizes for a ranking on a results page. AEO optimizes for being the answer itself. In practice, that changes what "good content" means: instead of writing toward a keyword and hoping to rank, you're writing toward a question and structuring the answer so an AI system can lift it cleanly: clear claims, real evidence, no burying the answer three paragraphs in. You're also tracking a different scoreboard. SEO success looks like position and clicks. AEO success looks like whether you're the one getting cited when someone asks ChatGPT or Perplexity the question, whether or not they ever land on your site.

**What's the impact of AI-powered search and answer engines on organic traffic?**

It's real and it's measurable. Pew Research, tracking 68,000 real search queries, found users clicked a result only 8% of the time when an AI summary appeared, versus 15% when it didn't, nearly half the rate. SparkToro estimates fewer than one in three Google searches still ends in a click to an external site. Ahrefs has reported click-through declines as steep as 58% for top-ranking pages when an AI Overview shows up above them. Most brands feel this as "traffic is dropping for no reason." It's rarely no reason. It's AI Overviews and chat-based search absorbing clicks that used to land on their site, though it's worth saying that's not the only reason. A lot of brands blame AI for a drop that's partly explained by basic SEO hygiene quietly falling apart.

**What strategies are brands using to improve visibility in AI-generated answers?**

Two that have actually worked for us. First, executive-led content: when we took an AI visibility startup, Search Party, from zero subscribers to a newsletter audience of 3,000+ in 90 days, the engine behind it was the founder's actual point of view, not generic content production. That same program earned the company top-4 visibility across high-intent AI prompts, putting them alongside category leaders despite having zero brand recognition going in. Second, writing in the language people actually use to ask AI systems questions, not the keywords they used to type into Google. We pull from Reddit threads, review sites, and sales call transcripts to find real buyer phrasing, then build content and tracking around that. Running this for Nooks increased their share of voice by 43% and citation rate by 37% in two months.

**What role do structured data, topical authority, expert content, and brand mentions play?**

Structured data is the most literally underrated piece. Schema markup is how you tell an AI system who you are, what you do, and how you relate to other entities, in language it doesn't have to guess at. Topical authority matters more than content volume: sustained, deep coverage of a specific area beats scattered posts across unrelated topics every time, because it gives AI systems a consistent pattern to recognize you by. Expert content, meaning content with a real named point of view behind it, performs better than generic explainer content because it's more citable: AI systems are increasingly favoring sources that read as a specific person or company saying something specific, not a wiki-style summary. And brand mentions matter even without a link. AI systems pick up on where you're discussed and how, including in places like Reddit and review sites, not just where you're linked.

**What metrics should businesses track for AEO success?**

Share of voice and citation rate, full stop. That's the AEO equivalent of rankings and traffic. Track which prompts you're showing up for and which you're not, not just an aggregate visibility score. If you can see referral or assisted traffic coming from AI platforms, watch that too, but don't expect it to look like traditional analytics — a lot of AI-driven visibility never produces a click at all, and that's not failure, that's the new shape of the funnel.

**What are common misconceptions about AEO?**

The biggest one: that AEO replaces SEO. It doesn't. If you chase AI citations and let your technical SEO and site fundamentals fall apart, your visibility suffers everywhere, AI included. The second: that more content means more citations. It's the opposite. AI systems cite the source that answered the question best, not the brand that published the most. Volume without a real point of view just adds noise.

**What's your prediction for the future relationship between SEO and AEO?**

They converge. The brands treating them as separate disciplines run by separate teams are going to lose to the ones running a single program built around one goal: being found and cited, whether that happens on a search results page or inside an AI-generated answer. The tools and tactics will keep evolving, but the underlying job doesn't change: be the source worth pointing to.
