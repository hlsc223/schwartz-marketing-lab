# Response to James — technology.org — "Startup Marketing in the AI Era"

Hi James,

Thanks for the opportunity — happy to weigh in. I'm Quinn Schwartz, founder of [Schwartz Marketing Lab](https://schwartzmarketinglab.com), where I work with B2B and SaaS companies on SEO, AEO, and content strategy. Answers below, use whatever's useful.

**What AI marketing use case has generated the biggest business impact for you?**

Synthesizing data at scale. When a client sees a sudden traffic drop, the instinct now is to blame AI Overviews or answer-engine cannibalization. Sometimes that's true. But just as often, their traditional SEO hygiene quietly fell apart while everyone was focused on the AI story. I'll upload reports from Ahrefs, Search Console, Screaming Frog, and GA4 into AI and have it cross-reference for gaps and technical issues a person would take days to find manually. That process alone has stopped the bleeding — and recovered lost traffic — for clients who assumed AI was the problem when it wasn't.

**What AI marketing trend are founders underestimating?**

The effect AI is having on thought leadership, and not in a good way. Open LinkedIn on any given day and a hundred new "thought leaders" have appeared overnight, all with the exact same voice and tone. Volume isn't the bottleneck anymore — trust is. Audiences can smell an AI-written post from a mile away, and more of them are tuning it out entirely. Founders who treat AI content production as a numbers game are training their audience to ignore them.

**What AI marketing trend are founders overestimating?**

"AI-native" as an identity. AI is a powerful tool, but it shouldn't be running your company. Lean on it too hard and you sterilize your team's creativity and end up in a sea of sameness with every other AI-native competitor. It also makes mistakes — frequently — and if those mistakes compound across your strategy, you end up in a death spiral where none of your numbers or goals make sense. Automate what makes sense to automate. Don't use AI as a crutch for thinking. I don't care what your investors say.

**How do you balance AI efficiency with authentic brand voice?**

You have to stay in control of the input. Don't hand AI a list of bullet points and expect your voice to come out the other side — it won't. Do a real brain dump, written or spoken, and let AI organize and clean it up. Then go back in and make it sound like you again. It doesn't have to be polished. The imperfections are usually what make it recognizably yours. The moment you let AI do your thinking instead of your typing, you've sterilized the thing that made people want to listen to you in the first place.

**What do you predict startup marketing will look like by 2028?**

The founders who figure out how to balance AI efficiency with an authentic voice will win. Anyone can spin up an app. Anyone can prompt ChatGPT into sounding smart. What's harder to fake is judgment, taste, and a point of view shaped by actually doing the work. By 2028, AI-generated marketing noise will be cheap, abundant, and easy to spot — which makes a real human voice behind a brand more valuable, not less.
