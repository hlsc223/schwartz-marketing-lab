<h2>How to choose the right AEO agency</h2>
<p>Industry experts used the following criteria to evaluate the agencies in this list. Readers should apply these same factors when comparing options and looking for the best AEO agency for their own team.</p>
<h3>Match scope to your actual gap</h3>
<p>Some agencies treat AEO as a standalone add-on bolted onto existing SEO or paid work. Others build it into a connected system spanning content, PR, and entity optimization. Before hiring, identify whether your gap is technical, such as missing schema, or strategic, such as weak third-party credibility, since the right scope depends entirely on which problem you actually have.</p>
<h3>Ask for citation evidence, not promises</h3>
<p>Any agency claiming AEO expertise should be able to show actual before-and-after citation data from past client work, not just a methodology slide. Ask which prompts they tracked, which AI platforms they monitored, and what changed. Vague answers about AI visibility improvements without specific prompts or platforms named are a warning sign worth taking seriously.</p>
<h3>Evaluate the content team, not just the strategy</h3>
<p>AEO strategy means little without writers who can translate it into content AI systems actually want to cite. Review writing samples directly, not case study summaries. Look for specificity, original data, and clear answers to real buyer questions rather than generic thought leadership that reads the same as every competitor's blog.</p>
<h3>Confirm how entity and schema work gets done</h3>
<p>Entity optimization requires structured data implementation, not just content advice. Ask whether the agency's team includes someone who can audit and implement schema markup directly, or whether that work gets handed off to your internal developers with instructions. The answer changes both the timeline and the actual workload on your side.</p>
<h3>Check whether PR and authority are part of the plan</h3>
<p>AI systems weight third-party validation heavily when deciding what to cite. An agency focused purely on owned content, without any plan for earned media or executive visibility, is solving half the problem. Ask directly how the agency builds the independent credibility signals AI systems use to decide what to trust.</p>

<h2>What to track after hiring an AEO agency</h2>
<p>The work does not end once a company hires an AEO agency. The right performance metrics measure both visibility and business impact, since traditional organic traffic alone no longer tells the full story. AI search requires a broader measurement framework built on multiple signals together.</p>
<h3>Share of voice across high-value prompts</h3>
<p>Keyword rankings only capture part of visibility now, since many buyer questions get answered directly inside an AI response. Track how often your brand appears across a fixed set of high-value prompts relative to named competitors. This share of voice metric reveals visibility that traditional rank tracking cannot see on its own.</p>
<h3>Citation rate inside AI-generated answers</h3>
<p>Being cited by name inside an AI-generated answer is often a stronger signal of authority than holding a top ranking position. Track citation rate across ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews for your core topics. Rising citations show that AI systems trust your content enough to reference it directly.</p>
<h3>Prompt-level visibility for your highest-value questions</h3>
<p>Aggregate visibility scores hide where a brand actually wins or loses, since one strong prompt can mask several weak ones. Track performance for the specific commercial and informational prompts that matter most to your buyers, not a blended average. This prompt-level view shows exactly where content gaps still exist.</p>
<h3>AI referral traffic and assisted visits</h3>
<p>AI referral traffic deserves monitoring, but many successful AI interactions never produce a click at all. Look for referral traffic from ChatGPT and Perplexity where your analytics tool reports it, while recognizing that AI discovery often happens without a single website visit. Treat this as one signal, not the whole picture.</p>
<h3>Self-reported attribution from new leads</h3>
<p>Every lead form should include a simple how did you hear about us field, since AI-driven discovery keeps growing in ways analytics tools cannot fully capture. Self-reported attribution often reveals AI influence that referral data misses entirely. Review these responses monthly alongside other tracked metrics for a fuller picture.</p>
<h3>Pipeline and revenue influence over time</h3>
<p>Visibility alone does not pay the bills, so the ultimate measure of AEO success is qualified pipeline and closed revenue. Connect AI visibility, branded search demand, sales conversations, and won deals into one shared view. No single metric proves AEO is working, but the full set together usually does.</p>
